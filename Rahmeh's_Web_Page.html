EVOLUTION OF AVATARS

AN ETERNAL EXISTENCE

DIGITAL AVATARS

A digital avatar is a graphical representation of a user or a user's character performing a certain task. It can be a two-dimensional icon, as seen on social networking platforms and forums, or a three-dimensional figure, as seen in games or virtual worlds.Despite its reputation as a form of entertainment, numerous organizations throughout the world have begun to recognize their value in industries such as healthcare, advertising, and soft-skills development(XpertVR, 2021).The term "avatar" comes from Hinduism, and it refers to a deity's "descent" into a terrestrial form.

Avatar bodies are created using modern technology to match human bodies, which are then mind-linked to these "Avatars" for remote control operation. The artificially intelligent avatar, which is a computer representation of a person that looks like a video or a photo but has intelligence, simulates how our brain conducts speech to provide human-like contact(Poondru Prithvinath Reddy, 2020).

TIMELINE

1970'S

The origins of Avatar can be traced back to the early 1970s, when two NASA employees named Steve Colley and Howard Palmer created MazeWar, a multiplayer game.

They interacted with other early computer users via the ARPANET, a forerunner to the Internet created by the Pentagon and a number of colleges.

Players were first represented by words or numbers as they moved through the maze. But, according to Bruce Damer, a graphical eyeball that traveled through the maze with its cyclops gaze focused in the direction it moved - or at the other players it was ready to shoot - was the first avatar (Inquirer Published Mar 18, 2007).

1980'S

Computers began to make their way into everyday houses. They may connect to a variety of computer networks and communicate exclusively with computers on the same network.

With a Commodore 64 computer, Lucas Films employees Chip Morningstar and Randy Farmer created the virtual environment Habitat.

Users created cartoon-like avatars by picking heads and bodies from a menu. They may then stroll around a digital space and converse with each other, with their remarks showing in chat boxes over their heads(Inquirer Published Mar 18, 2007).

1990'S

The avatar phenomenon truly took off when the numerous computer networks came together to become the Internet.Many multiplayer games and virtual social spaces were developed, but only a handful of them survived due to a lack of revenue.

Phillip Rosedale, the CEO and founder of Linden Labs, had a different perspective. "He had the impression that it was far too soon. The majority of individuals were still using dial-up connections "For many virtual-world applications, these are too slow(Inquirer Published Mar 18, 2007).

2003

Linden Labs developed Second Life, which spawned a slew of new businesses based on providing virtual-world residents goods and services(Inquirer Published Mar 18, 2007).
